History

Enzo Ferrari, born on February 20, 1898, in Modena, Italy, was the founder of the iconic luxury sports car manufacturer Ferrari. His life was dedicated to automobiles and racing, leaving an indelible mark on the automotive industry. Ferrari's passion for racing ignited during his youth when he attended car races with his father. In 1929, he founded Scuderia Ferrari, a racing team that initially operated as an Alfa Romeo racing division. Under his leadership, the team achieved numerous victories, establishing Ferrari's reputation as a formidable force in motorsports. In 1947, Enzo Ferrari unveiled the first car to bear his name, the Ferrari 125 S, marking the inception of Ferrari as an independent automobile manufacturer. Ferrari cars quickly gained renown for their performance, style, and engineering excellence.
Throughout his career, Enzo Ferrari remained deeply involved in motorsports, overseeing Ferrari's participation in Formula One racing. Ferrari became synonymous with Formula One success, winning multiple championships and becoming one of the most successful teams in the sport's history. Enzo Ferrari was known for his uncompromising dedication to perfection and his relentless pursuit of excellence. His visionary leadership and innovative engineering transformed Ferrari into a symbol of luxury, speed, and prestige. Beyond his contributions to the automotive world, Enzo Ferrari's legacy extends to his iconic quotes, such as "I build engines and attach wheels to them," which encapsulate his profound passion for automobiles. Enzo Ferrari passed away on August 14, 1988, but his name and legacy endure as Ferrari continues to produce some of the most coveted and revered sports cars in the world, embodying the spirit and vision of its founder.
